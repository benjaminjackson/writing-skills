# Archetype Bench

Exemplars, not a fixed cast. Every run should invent fresh names and specific voices — draw
loosely on the last decade or two of NY media for flavor, the way a real page-one meeting has
recognizable types without being any one real person. Pick 3-5 poles that create real tension
for *this specific pitch*, not a default set run on autopilot. If the pitch needs a pole not
listed here, invent one — this list exists to seed variety, not constrain it.

- **Virality / hook.** Will a reader actually click, share, forward this? Distrusts
  worthy-but-boring. Pushes for the sentence a stranger would repeat.

- **Prestige / craft-skeptic.** Demands a real idea, not just a topic or a tip. Allergic to
  service-y listicle energy. Wants tension, stakes, a reason a smart reader could disagree.

- **Rigor / numbers-cop.** Distrusts vibes and unexamined statistics. Wants the absolute number
  next to the relative one, the methodology, the denominator. Willing to kill a piece that's
  quantitatively hollow even if the story is emotionally compelling.

- **Service / utility.** Cares whether the reader can actually do something with this. Hunts
  for the fake-useful trap — advice that's technically true but out of the reader's real reach,
  or too small to matter. Insists any action be honestly labeled for who can actually take it.

- **Novelty / saturation.** Has read everything already published on this beat. Demands proof
  an angle is genuinely new, not a fresh coat of paint on a saturated take. Near-mandatory:
  this is the exact pole whose absence let an unoriginal angle through undetected the first
  time this workflow ran without it.

- **Institutional risk / standards.** Thinks about fairness, sourcing, conflicts of interest,
  legal exposure, whether a claim survives a fact-check. Maps to `managing-editor.md`'s fourth
  rubric question — reuse that framework's language here rather than reinventing it.

- **Contrarian audience.** Orthogonal to novelty — asks who *outside* a narrow, online, or
  professional bubble actually cares about this, and whether the piece is accidentally written
  only for people who already agree.

Deliberately not on this list: a "register/tone" persona to catch sales-copy, manufactured-
urgency language in the final draft. That failure is a writing-time defect, not something any
editor argues for or against mid-debate — putting it in the cast would just diffuse
responsibility for catching it onto "someone else's job."
