# Pitch Brief Template (Phase 6)

Shape, lifted from the real precedent at
`pitches/2026-06-27-cloud-carbon-renewable-lie/pitch-brief.md`. Adapt section count/order to
the cast actually seated for this pitch — this is the shape that worked, not a rigid form.

1. **The pitch in one line** — the claim, plainly stated, no sales register.
2. **Scope note(s)** — whatever the debate pinned down about who can actually act, who the
   real reader is, what's still open. Carry forward anything flagged during intake that never
   got resolved.
3. **How to use this brief** — tell the pitcher to draft from the per-persona sections below,
   then run the draft through: (a) the `managing-editor` agent as a gate — it has frameworks,
   not example pitches, and adapts if a target outlet is named; (b) real published examples
   (below).
4. **The theory** — what a pitch doc actually does and the standard shape editors read for
   (open → nut graf → outline/where it fits → proof → why-me/logistics). Keep this short; it's
   orientation, not padding.
5. **One section per seated persona**, each covering their lens on the pitch concretely,
   with a worked model line or two — not abstract advice. Example split from the precedent:
   a hook/open specialist, an idea/thesis specialist, a reader/service specialist, a
   news-value/proof specialist. Substitute whichever cast was actually seated this run.
6. **Real examples to study** — pull via `WebSearch`/`WebFetch`, not invented. Include, if
   relevant: The Open Notebook's pitch database (real, annotated successful pitches — the
   single best resource for this: theopennotebook.com/pitches), and genre analogues (real
   published pieces that pulled off a similar move to what this pitch is attempting).
7. **Fill-in template** — a copy-pasteable skeleton with the model answers slotted in from
   section 5, so the pitcher drafts over top of it rather than from a blank page.
8. **Still yours to bring** — the things only the pitcher can supply: their own voice/signature
   line, anything still needing verification, any reporting not yet done.
