# Intake Checklist

A menu, not a script. Silently check the pitcher's raw material against this list, then ask
only what's genuinely missing — 2-3 highest-value items at a time. Never work through this as
a form. If the pitcher says "just start," proceed and carry gaps forward as flagged
uncertainties in each persona's briefing packet rather than blocking.

0. **Raw material.** If nothing's been given yet — no research, no notes, not even a topic —
   this is the only thing to ask for before anything else.

1. **Reader / actor.** Who reads this, and — separately — who can actually *do* something
   about it? These are sometimes different people (e.g. a general reader who can only pass
   along a request, vs. the specific person who controls the lever the piece is about).

2. **Outlet.** Is there a target publication, or is this outlet-agnostic? If a target is
   named, check this repo's `pitch-guides/` directory for a matching file (e.g.
   `pitch-guides/vox.md`) — if one exists, load it before briefing the cast, and shape the
   final output to that outlet's real submission format.

3. **Evidence status.** What in the raw material is the pitcher's own research vs. already-
   published/reported fact vs. still speculative or unverified?

4. **Format / length.** Any known constraints, or fully open?

5. **Angle vs. material.** Does the pitcher already have a thesis, or just a page of research
   and a general direction? Don't assume an angle exists just because material does.

6. **Already rejected.** Any angles, companies, or framings the pitcher already considered and
   ruled out? Worth knowing so the panel doesn't re-propose them.

7. **Specificity of scope.** Are there named companies, regions, numbers — or is everything
   still generic? Vague scope is one of the most common things that surfaces mid-debate instead
   of up front, and it forces the room to re-litigate a position once it's pinned down.

8. **Competitive landscape.** Does the pitcher already know of similar existing coverage on
   this exact angle? This is an early, cheap pass at the novelty question the panel will also
   independently pressure-test in Phase 4 — redundancy here is intentional, not wasted effort.

9. **Why now.** Is there a known news peg, or is that something the room needs to establish?
