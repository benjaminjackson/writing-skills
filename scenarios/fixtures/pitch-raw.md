# Pitch Raw

Target outlet: Vox

Working headline: Why your first week at a new job still feels broken

Thesis: Companies have optimized hiring funnels but left onboarding as a pile of meetings, links, and manager memory. The story would explain why onboarding fails, what new employees actually need in week one, and why the fix is less about culture slogans than ownership and calendar design.

Evidence in hand:

- Interviews with three recent hires at mid-size software companies.
- A sample of onboarding calendars showing repeated company-overview meetings.
- HR leaders saying managers own onboarding in theory but rarely have a maintained checklist.

Reporting still needed:

- One workplace researcher.
- One operations leader who redesigned onboarding.
- A concrete example of a first-week checklist that reduced confusion.

Why now: Hybrid teams keep hiring across time zones, but many onboarding rituals still assume people can learn by overhearing office context.
